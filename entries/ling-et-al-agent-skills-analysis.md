## Why it's here

The only entry in this topic backed by actual data rather than
practitioner opinion. Analyzes over 40,000 publicly listed skills to
characterize how the ecosystem grew and what people actually built.

## Key claim or contribution

Documents extremely fast ecosystem growth (thousands of new skills
published per day at peak) and finds a large share of listings are
near-duplicate reposts rather than genuinely new skills — a real
signal that discovery/dedup tooling lags publishing volume. Also
reports that software engineering workflows dominate published skills,
while information-retrieval skills have the highest install counts
but remain comparatively undersupplied.

## Who should read/watch this

Anyone designing an ingestion or dedup pipeline for a skills/knowledge
repo — this paper is direct evidence for why dedup discipline matters,
not just a nice-to-have.
