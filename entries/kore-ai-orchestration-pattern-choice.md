## Why it's here

Weaker technical depth than the other entries in this topic, but fills
a real gap: a simplified framing aimed at less technical
decision-makers, useful when you need to explain an architecture
choice to a non-engineer stakeholder.

## Key claim or contribution

Simplifies the pattern space into three categories: Supervisor pattern
(centralized command and control, a hierarchical architecture with one
central orchestrator coordinating all agent interactions), Adaptive
agent network pattern (decentralized collaboration), and Custom
pattern (programmatic flexibility for cases the first two don't fit).
Frames pattern choice as matching an organization's technical
maturity, not just the technical problem.

## Who should read/watch this

Anyone who needs to justify an architecture decision to a
non-technical stakeholder or exec — this is the "explain it simply"
reference the more technical entries in this topic don't provide.
