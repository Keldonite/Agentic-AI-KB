## Why it's here

Academic survey covering graph-structured agent memory broadly,
complementing the Hindsight paper's benchmark-focused approach with a
wider literature view.

## Key claim or contribution

Surveys graph-based memory approaches for agents (naming systems
including Mem0, A-MEM, Zep, and G-memory) and observes that
graph-structured memory tends to emerge naturally as agents accumulate
relational insight over time — linking related concepts, tracking
dependencies between subtasks, and recording causal relationships
inferred through interaction, rather than being designed in from the
start. Notes that graph-based agent memory systems often construct,
extend, or reorganize their internal graph structure continuously
during operation, not just at setup.

## Who should read/watch this

Anyone wanting the broader literature landscape behind graph-based
memory systems, as context for interpreting the more benchmark-focused
Hindsight paper in this topic.
